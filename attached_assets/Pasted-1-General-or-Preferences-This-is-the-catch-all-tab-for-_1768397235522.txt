1. General (or Preferences)
This is the "catch-all" tab for high-level configurations that don't fit neatly into other specific categories.
Language & Region: Options to change the app language, time zone, or date formats.
Start-up Behavior: Settings for what happens when the app launches (e.g., "Open last used project" vs. "Open dashboard").
Accessibility: Toggles for high contrast, font scaling, or screen reader optimization.
2. Account (or Profile)
This section focuses on the user's identity and subscription status.
Profile Information: Fields to edit name, email, avatar/photo, and bio.
Subscription/Billing: Details on current plan (Free vs. Pro), payment methods, and billing history.
Export/Delete Data: Options to download account data or permanently delete the account.
3. Notifications
Almost all modern applications require a dedicated section to manage alerts to prevent "notification fatigue."
Push vs. Email: Separate toggles for mobile push notifications, email digests, and SMS.
Activity Types: Granular controls for what triggers a notification (e.g., "New comments," "System updates," "Marketing emails").
Do Not Disturb: Scheduling quiet hours or muting notifications temporarily.
4. Privacy & Security
This is increasingly critical for user trust and compliance.
Password Management: Options to change the password or enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
Connected Devices: A list of active sessions or devices currently logged in.
Permissions: Managing what the app can access (e.g., Camera, Microphone, Location, Contacts).
Visibility: Controls for who can see the user's profile or activity (e.g., Public, Friends Only, Private).
5. Appearance (or Display)
Focuses on the visual interface and customization.
Theme: The standard Light Mode, Dark Mode, or "Sync with System" options.
Layout Density: Options for "Compact" vs. "Comfortable" views (common in data-heavy apps).
Font Size: Text scaling options for better readability.
6. Integrations (or Connections)
For productivity or social apps that talk to other services.
Connected Apps: Linking accounts like Google, Slack, or Microsoft 365.
API Keys: For developer-focused applications, this is where users generate keys to access the backend programmatically.
7. Help & About
Often placed at the bottom or as a separate tab.
Version Info: The current build number (essential for troubleshooting).
Terms & Privacy Policy: Legal documentation.
Support: Links to FAQs, knowledge bases, or "Contact Us" forms.
